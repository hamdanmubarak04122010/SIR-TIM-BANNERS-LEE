Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee, OM, KBE, FRS, FREng, FRSA, DFBCS, RDI, also known as TimBL, is an English computer scientist best known as the inventor of the World Wide Web. He is a Professorial Fellow of Computer Science at the University of Oxford and a professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Born: June 8, 1955 (age 67 years), London, United Kingdom Education: The Queen's College (1973–1976), Emanuel School (1969–1973), Sheen Mount Primary School Awards: Millennium Technology Prize, Japan Prize, Turing Award, MORE Siblings: Mike Berners-Lee Dates knighted: 1997, 2004 Spouse: Rosemary Leith (m. 2014), Nancy Carlson (m. 1990–2011) Parents: Conway Berners-Lee, Mary Lee Woods
